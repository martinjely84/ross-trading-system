1 The Very Basics

Before we explain the bot, here are the two things you need to understand about stocks.

What is a stock?

A stock is a tiny slice of ownership in a company. When a company like Apple goes public, it splits itself into millions of tiny pieces called shares. You can buy a share for, say, $180 — and if more people want to own Apple, the price of your share goes up. If fewer people want it, the price drops.

The bot trades stocks that are moving fast — it buys them, waits a few minutes for the price to go up, then sells them for a profit.

2 What is a Gap?

Stock markets close at 4pm and reopen at 9:30am the next morning. Overnight, news can come out — earnings reports, FDA drug approvals, CEO announcements — and by the time the market opens, a stock's price can jump significantly. This jump is called a gap up.

Real example
A small biotech company announces that the FDA approved their new drug at 7am. The stock closed yesterday at $3.00. When the market opens at 9:30am it immediately starts trading at $5.50 — a gap up of 83%. Traders rush to buy it, pushing the price even higher.

Why do gaps matter?

When a stock gaps up significantly, it creates a surge of buying activity right at the open (9:30am). The momentum can carry the price up 20%, 50%, even 100% within the first hour. The bot is designed to catch this momentum wave in the first 90 minutes of trading — before it fades.

3 The Strategy: Gap and Go

The bot follows a strategy pioneered by professional trader Ross Cameron of Warrior Trading. It is called Gap and Go. The idea is simple:

  1. Find a stock that has gapped up sharply on strong news overnight.
  2. Wait for the market to open and confirm that buyers are still pushing the price higher.
  3. Enter the trade at a key price level (a breakout).
  4. Let the momentum carry the price up for a defined profit target.
  5. Exit before the momentum runs out.

The bot does all of this automatically — scanning hundreds of stocks pre-market, shortlisting the best candidates, and then executing trades in real-time.

Why this strategy works
Human emotions drive stock prices in the short term. When a stock surges on big news, fear of missing out (FOMO) causes more and more buyers to pile in, pushing the price higher in a predictable wave. The bot is designed to ride this wave systematically — without emotion.

4 How the Bot Picks Stocks Each Morning

From 4am onwards (before the US market opens), the bot runs a pre-market scan. It looks at hundreds of stocks and filters them down using strict criteria:

1
Gap size The stock must have gapped up at least 10–20% from yesterday's close. Small gaps are ignored — not enough momentum.
2
Trading volume Hundreds of thousands of shares must already be trading pre-market. Low volume means no one cares — the move won't last.
3
Float (supply of shares) The bot prefers stocks with a small float — meaning there are fewer shares available to buy. When demand surges but supply is limited, prices spike harder.
4
Catalyst (the reason) There must be a real reason for the gap — FDA approval, earnings beat, merger news, government contract. Stocks that gap up for no reason don't hold their gains.
5
Relative volume Is today's volume unusually high compared to a normal day? The bot requires at least 2–3× the normal volume, confirming this is a significant event.

The result is a shortlist of 1–4 stocks — the watchlist for the day. These are sent to you via Telegram each morning at 9:15am ET.

Not every trade opportunity is equally good. The bot rates each setup on a scale:

This system means the bot automatically bets more on the best opportunities and less on weaker ones.

7 Risk Management — How Losses Are Controlled

Making money on winners is only half the equation. The other half is making sure losing trades don't wipe out the gains. The bot has several built-in protections.

Per-trade stop loss

Every single trade has a pre-set stop loss — a price level where the bot automatically exits if the trade goes wrong. The maximum loss on any single trade is 1% of the account.

Example
Account value: $95,000  →  Max loss per trade: $950. If a stock drops to the stop loss price, the bot sells instantly — the loss is capped and the account lives to fight another day.

Daily loss limit

If the bot loses more than 2% of the account in a single day, all trading automatically stops for the rest of the day. There is no way to override this — it is a hard lock. This prevents a bad morning from becoming a catastrophic day.

Partial sells — locking in profit

Rather than selling everything at once, the bot exits in three stages (T1, T2, Runner). This means:

  • Profit is secured early, even if the stock reverses
  • The stop loss moves to breakeven after T1 — the trade becomes risk-free
  • A small "runner" position is left open to capture bigger moves

11am hard cutoff

All positions are force-closed at 11am ET regardless. Momentum stocks are most predictable in the first 90 minutes. After 11am, trading becomes more random and the edge disappears — so the bot stops.

8 The Maths: Why It Works Over Time

Here is the key insight behind this strategy. You do not need to win every trade. You just need your winners to be bigger than your losers.

Tough day 4 2 +$500 -$450 +$100
Bad day 3 1 +$300 -$400 -$500
Great day 3 3 +$1,200 — +$3,600

Even with a 60% win rate (6 wins out of 10 trades), if each win averages +2R and each loss is -1R, the expected profit per 10 trades is:

Expected value calculation
(6 wins × +2R) − (4 losses × 1R) = +12R − 4R = +8R net profit per 10 trades

If 1R = $500 risk per trade, that is +$4,000 per 10 trades — on average.
